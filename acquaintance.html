Engineering Acquaintance Party, A Success

Posted Jan 7, 2014 by Joseph Magdalera

LA HERENCIA - As the new academic year began, dozens of fresh faces in the College of Engineering and Technology can be seen strolling around the SE corridors, trying to cope with their new environment. To welcome these perplexed frosh to the engineering family, a big day was organized to give them a big-bang start.

Last August 25, 2013, Sunday, the engineering student council spearheaded by Governor Ryan Jake Pisueña held the Acquaintance party E-nugyaw 2013 at La Herencia, Brgy. Bata, Bacolod City.

Despite holding it on a Sunday, Pisueña said he believed that the affair was a huge success, and added that the feedbacks he received from the freshmen were mostly positive.

“The event really brought the essence of being one as a college. Stronger interactions among the students were developed. The goal of welcoming the frosh to the engineering family was undoubtedly achieved. Let us keep this fire burning!” he said.

The governor also thanked the engineering council, council moderator, dean, faculty, clubs and all the people in organizing and supporting the said occasion.
